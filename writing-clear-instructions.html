Writing clear instructions on mbta.com

Content on mbta.com helps guide transit riders through their travel. Here's how I write instructions, knowing that most people scan and skim webpages for the info they need.

Check for an email confirmation

Working with a first draft from stakeholders, I helped write a more informative confirmation message for mbta.com's customer support form.

Previous copy:

Thank you for your feedback. Your comments have been sent to our Customer Support team.

Now:

Thank you for submitting your feedback. Our Customer Support team will review your comments soon.
If you provided your contact info, check your email for a confirmation with an incident number. If you don't receive a confirmation within 10 minutes, try submitting your feedback again or call us at xxx-xxx-xxxx.

Writing methods:

  • Recall exact terminology from the form [contact info]. This helps riders clearly determine if the instruction applies to them.
  • Use verbs like check, try, and call. This gives riders explicit and active steps they can follow.
  • Write precise time info [within 10 minutes]. This gives riders reassurance and a clear threshold for action.

Find the right ski train

Live webpage: Wachusett Mountain

I worked with stakeholders to write more detailed information about ski trains for the 2024 – 25 season.

Previous copy:

Each train will feature a specifically modified coach equipped with racks for ski and snowboard equipment.

Updated for the 2024 – 25 season:

The following Fitchburg Line trains will feature a specifically modified coach equipped with racks for ski and snowboard equipment:
  • Saturdays: Train 1403 to Wachusett and Train 1412 to North Station
  • Sundays: Train 2403 to Wachusett and Train 2412 to North Station

Writing methods:

  • Use bulleted lists. This makes important info stand out visually.
  • Frontload date/time info [Saturdays, Sundays]. This helps riders narrow down which point is relevant to them.
  • Write precise location info [to Wachusett, to North Station]. This helps riders know which direction to look for on a timetable.

Download the mobile app

Live webpage: MBTA Go

I wrote guidance to let riders know what they need to get the MBTA's mobile app.

To download on the App Store, you'll need an iPhone with iOS 16.4 or later.

Writing methods:

  • Frontload to + infinitive clauses. This immediately indicates who should keep reading (iOS users who use the App Store) and why (if they want to download the app).
