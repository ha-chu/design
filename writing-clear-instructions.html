Writing clear instructions on mbta.com

Content on mbta.com helps guide transit riders through actions to take and train schedules to follow. Here's how I write instructions, knowing that most people scan and skim webpages for the info they need.

Check for an email confirmation

Live webpage: Customer Support

I rewrote the confirmation message for mbta.com's customer support form. It gives more instruction on how to check that your form submission went through, and what to do if it didn't.

Previous copy:

Thank you for your feedback. Your comments have been sent to our Customer Support team.

Now:

Thank you for submitting your feedback. Our Customer Support team will review your comments soon.
If you provided your contact info, check your email for a confirmation with an incident number. If you don't receive a confirmation within 10 minutes, try submitting your feedback again or call us at xxx-xxx-xxxx.

Writing methods:

  1. Frontloaded "if" statement. This indicates who should continue reading and who can ignore the rest.
  2. Verbs like check, try, and call. This gives riders explicit steps they can follow.
  3. Precise time info: within 10 minutes. This gives riders reassurance and a clear threshold for action.

Download the mobile app

Live webpage: MBTA Go

I wrote guidance to let riders know what they need to get the MBTA's mobile app.

To download on the App Store, you'll need an iPhone with iOS 16.4 or later.

Writing methods:

  1. Frontloaded to + infinitive clause. This immediately indicates who should keep reading (iOS users who use the App Store) and why (if they want to download the app).
